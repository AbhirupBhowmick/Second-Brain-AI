The concept of "Frozen Light" dictates that depth is perceived through layered, translucent surfaces rather than hard drop shadows. This approach creates an atmospheric, premium aesthetic that feels both futuristic and grounded.

When implementing these patterns, it's crucial to balance visibility and legibility. The background canvas must remain dark enough (#0a0e1a) to allow the primary luminous accents to shine through.

"Never use opaque solid backgrounds on floating elements. Keep borders subtle — luminous, not structural."

Core Structural Patterns

Elevation is simulated by increasing opacity and blur intensity simultaneously. Layer 1 utilizes a 60% opacity fill with a 16px blur radius. As elements move 'closer' to the user (Layer 2), opacity increases to 75% alongside a 24px blur.
